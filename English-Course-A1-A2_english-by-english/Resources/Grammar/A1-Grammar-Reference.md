## 1. The Verb 'to be'

### What is 'to be'?
The verb 'to be' is the most important verb in English. We use it to talk about:
- Who we are (name, job)
- How we feel
- Where we are from
- Descriptions (age, nationality)

### Present Tense Forms
| Subject | Verb | Example | Arabic Translation |
|---------|------|---------|--------------------|
| I       | am   | I am Sarah. | أنا سارة. |
| You     | are  | You are from Egypt. | أنت من مصر. |
| He      | is   | He is a teacher. | هو مدرس. |
| She     | is   | She is 20 years old. | هي بعمر 20 سنة. |
| It      | is   | It is a book. | هذا كتاب. |
| We      | are  | We are students. | نحن طلاب. |
| They    | are  | They are from Saudi. | هم من السعودية. |

### Contractions (Short Forms)
| Full | Contraction |
|------|-------------|
| I am | I'm |
| You are | You're |
| He is | He's |
| She is | She's |
| It is | It's |
| We are | We're |
| They are | They're |

### Negative Form
Add **not** after the verb:
- I am not a doctor.
- He is not from Canada.
- They are not teachers.

### Questions with 'to be'
Put the verb **before** the subject:
- Am I late?
- Are you from Morocco?
- Is she your sister?

**Wh- Questions:**
- What is your name?
- Where are you from?
- How old are you?

## 2. Present Simple Tense

### Use
To talk about:
- Habits and routines
- Things we do regularly
- Facts and general truths

### Form
- For **I, You, We, They**: Use the base verb
- For **He, She, It**: Add -s or -es

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I | work | I work in a shop. |
| You | work | You work in a shop. |
| He / She / It | works | He works in a shop. |
| We | work | We work in a shop. |
| They | work | They work in a shop. |

### Spelling Rules for -s/-es
| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Add -s | work → works |
| Add -es (ch, sh, s, x, o) | teach → teaches, go → goes |
| y → ies (consonant + y) | study → studies |

### Negative Form
Use **don't** or **doesn't**:
- I don't like coffee.
- He doesn't live in Paris.

### Questions
Use **Do** or **Does**:
- Do you speak English?
- Does she work here?

## 3. Present Continuous Tense

### Use
To talk about actions happening **right now** (at the moment of speaking).

### Form
Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

### Spelling Rules for -ing
| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Add -ing | work → working |
| e → ing (drop e) | write → writing |
| Double consonant (short vowel + consonant) | sit → sitting |

### Examples
- I am eating.
- She is reading a book.
- They are playing football.

### Negative Form
Add **not** after am/is/are:
- I am not eating.
- She isn't reading.

### Questions
Put am/is/are before the subject:
- Are you eating?
- What is she doing?

---

## 4. Future with 'Going to'

### Use
To talk about future plans and intentions.

### Form
Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb

### Examples
- I am going to study tomorrow.
- She is going to buy a new car.
- We are going to travel next week.

### Negative Form
Add **not** after am/is/are:
- I am not going to study.

### Questions
Put am/is/are before the subject:
- Are you going to study?
- What are you going to do?

## 5. Articles: a, an, the

### 'a' and 'an' (Indefinite Articles)
Use for non-specific, singular things:
- **a** before a consonant sound: a book, a car
- **an** before a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u): an apple, an orange

### 'the' (Definite Article)
Use for specific things:
- The book on the table.
- The sun, the moon.

### No Article
Use for:
- Plural general things: I like cats.
- Countries, cities, languages: I live in Egypt.

## 6. Possessives

### Possessive Adjectives
Use before a noun:

| English | Arabic | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| my | لي | my father |
| your | لك | your mother |
| his | له | his brother |
| her | لها | her sister |
| its | له (غير عاقل) | its name |
| our | لنا | our family |
| their | لهم | their house |

### Possessive 's
Add **'s** to show something belongs to someone:
- Ahmed's book
- Sara's mother
- The children's toys

For plural nouns ending with **s**, just add **'**:
- The boys' room
- My parents' house

## 8. Questions

### Yes/No Questions
- With 'to be': Am I late? / Are you happy?
- With Present Simple: Do you like it? / Does she work here?

### Wh- Questions
Ask for information:
- What (thing): What is your name?
- Where (place): Where do you live?
- When (time): When is your birthday?
- Who (person): Who is that?
- Why (reason): Why are you sad?
- How (way/condition): How are you?

---

## 9. Modals: Can, Have to, Want, Would Like

### Can
- **Ability**: I can swim.
- **Permission**: You can go now.

### Have to
- **Obligation**: I have to work.

### Want
- **Desire (informal)**: I want a coffee.

### Would like
- **Desire (polite)**: I would like some water.

---

## 10. Adjectives

### Descriptive Adjectives
Use to describe nouns. They don't change for plural/gender:
- a red car
- two red cars

### Order of Adjectives
Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material:
- A nice small old red Italian leather bag

## 11. Numbers and Ordinal Numbers

### Cardinal Numbers
1 - one, 2 - two, 3 - three, ..., 10 - ten, 11 - eleven, ..., 20 - twenty, ..., 100 - one hundred

### Ordinal Numbers
1st - first, 2nd - second, 3rd - third, 4th - fourth, ..., 20th - twentieth
