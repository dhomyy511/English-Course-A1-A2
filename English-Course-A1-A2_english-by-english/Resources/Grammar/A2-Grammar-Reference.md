## 1. Past Simple Tense

### Use
To talk about completed actions in the past.

### Regular Verbs
Add -ed:
- work → worked
- play → played
- live → lived

### Irregular Verbs
Learn the past forms (see the table at the end).

### Form
- **Affirmative**: Subject + verb (past)
  - I worked yesterday.
  - She went to Paris.
- **Negative**: Subject + didn't + base verb
  - I didn't work.
  - She didn't go.
- **Question**: Did + subject + base verb?
  - Did you work?
  - Did she go?

## 2. Comparatives and Superlatives

### Comparatives (Compare two things)
| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Short (1 syllable) | Add -er | small → smaller |
| Short (1 syllable, vowel + consonant) | Double consonant + -er | big → bigger |
| Ends with e | Add -r | nice → nicer |
| 2 syllables, ends with y | y → ier | happy → happier |
| Long (2+ syllables) | more + adjective | beautiful → more beautiful |

### Superlatives (Compare 3+ things)
| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Short | Add -est | small → smallest |
| Short (double consonant) | Double consonant + -est | big → biggest |
| Ends with e | Add -st | nice → nicest |
| 2 syllables, y → iest | happy → happiest |
| Long | most + adjective | beautiful → most beautiful |
| Irregular | good → best, bad → worst | |

## 3. There is / There are

### Use
To say that something exists.

### Form
- **Singular**: There is + singular noun
  - There is a kitchen.
- **Plural**: There are + plural noun
  - There are two bedrooms.
- **Negative**: There isn't / There aren't
- **Question**: Is there / Are there?

---

## 4. Quantifiers: Some, Any, Much, Many, A lot of

### Some
Use in affirmative sentences:
- I have some books.

### Any
Use in negative sentences and questions:
- I don't have any books.
- Do you have any books?

### Much
Use with uncountable nouns (in questions/negatives):
- How much money do you have?
- I don't have much time.

### Many
Use with countable nouns (in questions/negatives):
- How many books do you have?
- I don't have many friends.

### A lot of / Lots of
Use in affirmative sentences (countable or uncountable):
- I have a lot of books.
- I have a lot of money.

## 5. Adverbs of Frequency

| Adverb | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| always | 100% | I always get up at 7. |
| usually | 80% | I usually eat breakfast. |
| often | 60% | I often go to the gym. |
| sometimes | 40% | I sometimes watch TV. |
| rarely | 20% | I rarely eat fast food. |
| never | 0% | I never smoke. |

**Position**: Before the main verb, after 'to be'.

## 6. Present Perfect (Basic)

### Use
To talk about experiences in life (when we don't say when):
- I have visited Paris.
- She has never tried sushi.

### Form
- **Affirmative**: Subject + have/has + past participle
- **Negative**: Subject + haven't/hasn't + past participle
- **Question**: Have/Has + subject + past participle?

### Common Questions
- Have you ever...?
- Has she ever...?

## 7. Prepositions of Time

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| in | Months, years, seasons | in January, in 2023, in summer |
| on | Days, dates | on Monday, on January 5th |
| at | Specific time | at 3 o'clock, at night |
