## 📖 Section 2: Grammar - 'Too' and 'Enough'

### 'Too' (more than needed)
- Subject + verb + too + adjective:
  - This bag is too heavy.
  - I'm too tired to go out.

### 'Enough' (as much as needed)
- Before a noun: enough + noun
  - I have enough money.
  - There's enough food.
- After an adjective: adjective + enough
  - She's old enough to drive.
  - The room is big enough.

---

## 📖 Section 3: Grammar - 'Should' & 'Shouldn't' (Advice)

Use 'should' to give advice:
- You should go to bed early.
- You shouldn't eat too much junk food.

**Form**:
- Should + base verb
- Shouldn't + base verb (negative)

---

## 📖 Section 4: Grammar - Present Perfect (Basic Experience)

Use Present Perfect to talk about life experiences (when you don't say the exact time):

**Form**:
- Have/Has + Past Participle
  - I have visited Paris.
  - She has never tried sushi.
  - Have you ever been to London?

**Common Irregular Past Participles**:
- be → been
- go → gone / been
- see → seen
- eat → eaten
- drink → drunk
- do → done
- read → read
- write → written
- meet → met

## 📖 Section 5: Dialogue - At the Doctor's

**Doctor:** Hello! What's the matter?  
**Sarah:** I feel sick. I have a headache and a sore throat.  
**Doctor:** Have you taken your temperature?  
**Sarah:** Yes, I have a fever.  
**Doctor:** Open your mouth... OK. You have a bad cold. You should drink lots of water and rest. You shouldn't go to school for two days.  
**Sarah:** OK. Should I take medicine?  
**Doctor:** Yes, you can take these pills. Take one pill every 8 hours.  
**Sarah:** Thank you, doctor!  
**Doctor:** You're welcome. Get well soon!

## 📖 Section 6: Reading - My Healthy Lifestyle

Hi! My name is Ali. I try to live a healthy lifestyle! I usually get up early and go for a run in the park. I eat lots of fruit and vegetables. I don't eat too much junk food - it's bad for you! I drink at least 8 glasses of water a day. I usually sleep 8 hours every night.

Last month, I had the flu. I was very sick! The doctor said I should stay in bed and rest. I should have been more careful - I had been too tired! Have you ever had the flu? It's not fun!

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What does Ali do to stay healthy?
2. What does he eat?
3. How much water does he drink?
4. What was wrong with him last month?
5. What did the doctor say?

## 📖 Section 7: Writing - Healthy Habits

### Exercise: Write about your healthy habits or give advice

Choose one topic:
1. My healthy lifestyle (what you do to stay healthy)
2. Advice for someone who is sick (use should / shouldn't)
3. A time you were sick (use Past Simple)

**Example (Healthy Lifestyle):**
Hello! My name is Fatima. I try to be healthy. I exercise three times a week. I eat fruit and vegetables every day. I drink lots of water. I sleep 8 hours every night. I think this is enough to stay healthy!

**Example (Advice):**
If you have a cold: you should stay in bed and rest. You should drink lots of water and orange juice. You shouldn't go to work or school. You should take medicine if you have a fever.

## 📖 Section 9: Speaking - Health & Advice

### Role Play: At the Doctor's
**Patient:** Doctor, I don't feel well.  
**Doctor:** What's the matter?  
**Patient:** I have _______.  
**Doctor:** Have you _______?  
**Patient:** Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.  
**Doctor:** OK. You should _______. You shouldn't _______.  
**Patient:** OK, thank you, doctor!

### Useful Phrases
- What's the matter? / What's wrong?
- I have a headache.
- I feel sick.
- Have you ever...?
- You should...
- You shouldn't...
- I'm too tired.
- It's good enough.

### Exercise 1: 'Too' or 'Enough'
1. The tea is (too / enough) hot to drink.
2. I'm not old (too / enough) to drive.
3. Is there (too / enough) food for everyone?

2. You (should / shouldn't) exercise every day.
3. You (should / shouldn't) go to bed late.
4. You (should / shouldn't) drink lots of water.
