## 📖 Section 2: Grammar - Comparatives & Superlatives

Use comparatives to compare two things, superlatives to compare three or more!

### Comparatives
| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Short (1 syllable) | Add -er | small → smaller, big → bigger |
| Ends with e | Add -r | nice → nicer |
| 2 syllables, ends with y | y → ier | happy → happier |
| Long (2+ syllables) | more + adjective | beautiful → more beautiful |
| Irregular | good → better, bad → worse, far → farther/further | good → better |

### Superlatives
| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Short (1 syllable) | Add -est | small → smallest, big → biggest |
| Ends with e | Add -st | nice → nicest |
| 2 syllables, ends with y | y → iest | happy → happiest |
| Long (2+ syllables) | most + adjective | beautiful → most beautiful |
| Irregular | good → best, bad → worst, far → farthest/furthest | good → best |

**Important**: Use 'the' before superlatives!
- The Nile is the longest river in the world.
- Cairo is the biggest city in Egypt.

## 📖 Section 3: Grammar - Prepositions of Time

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| in | Months (in January), years (in 2023), seasons (in summer), centuries (in the 20th century) | in summer, in 2024 |
| on | Days (on Monday), dates (on January 5th), special days (on Christmas Day) | on Friday, on 15th March |
| at | Specific time (at 3 o'clock), times of day (at night), meals (at lunchtime), festivals (at Christmas) | at 7 PM, at night |

## 📖 Section 5: Dialogue - Asking for Directions

**Sarah:** Excuse me! Can you help me? I'm lost.  
**Ali:** Of course! Where do you want to go?  
**Sarah:** I want to go to the museum. Is it far from here?  
**Ali:** No, it's not far. Go straight on, then turn left at the traffic lights. Go past the park. The museum is on the right, opposite the library.  
**Sarah:** Thank you! How long does it take to walk there?  
**Ali:** About 10 minutes.  
**Sarah:** Great! Thanks for your help.  
**Ali:** You're welcome!

## 📖 Section 6: Reading - My Trip to Alexandria

Last summer, my family and I went to Alexandria. We traveled by car. It took about two hours. We stayed in a small hotel near the sea. The hotel was cheaper than the big hotels, but it was very comfortable!

On the first day, we went to the beach. The sea was beautiful! We swam and played volleyball. On the second day, we visited the Bibliotheca Alexandrina. It's the biggest library in the Middle East! On the third day, we went shopping and bought souvenirs. We had a great time! I think Alexandria is one of the most beautiful cities in Egypt.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. Where did Fatima go last summer?
2. How did she travel there?
3. Where did she stay?
4. What did she do there?
5. Did she have a good time?

## 📖 Section 7: Writing - Plan a Trip

### Exercise: Write about a past trip or plan a future trip

Choose one:
1. A trip you went on (using Past Simple)
2. A trip you want to go on (using 'going to' or 'want to')

**Example (Past Trip):**
Last year, I went to Luxor with my family. We traveled by train. We visited the Karnak Temple and the Valley of the Kings. It was amazing! The temples are older than I thought. We took a lot of photos.

**Example (Future Trip):**
Next summer, I'm going to go to Hurghada. I'm going to travel by bus with my friends. We're going to stay in a hotel near the beach. We're going to swim and go diving. I think it's going to be the best holiday ever!

## 📖 Section 9: Speaking - Give Directions

### Role Play: Ask for Directions
**Person A:** Excuse me! Can you help me?  
**Person B:** Yes, of course! Where do you want to go?  
**Person A:** I want to go to the _______.  
**Person B:** OK! Go _______, then _______. It's _______.  
**Person A:** Thank you very much!  
**Person B:** You're welcome!

### Useful Phrases
- Excuse me!
- Can you help me?
- I'm lost.
- Where is...?
- Go straight on.
- Turn left / right.
- It's on the left / right.
- It's opposite...
- How long does it take?
